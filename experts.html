Frequently asked Questions

Quartz countertops are engineered surfaces made by combining natural quartz crystals with resins and pigments. They typically consist of about 90-95% quartz and 5-10% resin binders and pigments for color and durability.

Yes, quartz countertops are highly durable. They are resistant to scratches, stains, and cracks, making them a great choice for kitchens and bathrooms. However, they are not heat-proof, so using trivets for hot pans is recommended.

To clean quartz countertops, use a soft cloth or sponge with mild dish soap and water. Avoid abrasive cleaners or harsh chemicals, as they may damage the surface.

Quartz countertops can resist moderate heat but can get damaged by extreme temperatures. It’s best to use hot pads or trivets to protect the surface from hot pots, pans, or appliances.

Minor chips and scratches can often be repaired using specialized quartz repair kits. However, extensive damage may require professional assistance or replacement of the affected section.

No, quartz countertops do not need sealing because they are non-porous. This feature makes them stain-resistant and easy to maintain compared to natural stones like granite or marble.

Quartz countertops are considered environmentally friendly as they are made using abundant natural quartz and recycled materials. However, the production process does involve synthetic resins and energy consumption.

Quartz countertops are more uniform in appearance, easier to maintain (no sealing required), and non-porous, making them more hygienic. Granite, on the other hand, is 100% natural, with unique patterns and higher heat resistance.

It is not recommended to cut directly on quartz countertops as it can dull your knives and potentially leave minor marks. Always use a cutting board to protect the surface.

The cost of quartz countertops varies depending on the brand, color, and thickness. On average, they range between $50 and $120 per square foot, including installation.
